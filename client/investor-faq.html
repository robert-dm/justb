Business Model

How does justB make money?

justB generates revenue through three primary streams:

  1. Commission Model (Primary): We take 15-20% commission on every order processed through the platform. This is our main revenue driver and follows the proven marketplace model used by DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Airbnb.
  2. Subscription Plans (Growth Phase): Premium features for both tourists (favorites, priority booking) and providers (featured listings, analytics tools).
  3. Advertising (Future): Local tourism partners and food brands can advertise to our targeted tourist audience.
What are your unit economics?

Our unit economics are strong and improve with scale:

  • Average Order Value: $22-26
  • Platform Commission: 18% average = $3.96-4.68 per order
  • Customer Acquisition Cost: $25 (Year 1) ‚Üí $12 (Year 3)
  • Lifetime Value: $99-117 (based on 5 trips over 2-3 years)
  • LTV:CAC Ratio: 4:1 initially, improving to 10:1 at scale

Positive unit economics are achieved after a tourist's first return visit, making the model sustainable from early on.

How do you compete with Uber Eats and DoorDash?

We don't compete directly - we serve a different use case:

  • Vacation-Specific: Tourists want to pre-book breakfast for their entire stay, not make daily ad-hoc orders
  • Recurring Orders: Our platform is built for multi-day scheduled deliveries
  • Local Authenticity: We curate local breakfast experiences, not just any restaurant
  • Pre-Trip Booking: Tourists book before arriving, reducing friction
  • Vertical Specialization: We're focused exclusively on the vacation breakfast niche

Think of us as "the breakfast layer" for vacation rentals, similar to how hotel booking sites don't compete with general travel platforms.

Why can't Airbnb or Booking.com build this themselves?

While they could, there are several reasons why we have an advantage:

  1. Focus: We're 100% dedicated to this problem. For Airbnb, it's a small feature among many priorities.
  2. Provider Relationships: Building a network of local breakfast providers requires specialized outreach and onboarding.
  3. Food Safety & Compliance: Different regulatory environment than property rentals.
  4. Acquisition Target: If successful, we become an attractive acquisition for these platforms.
  5. Partner Opportunity: We can integrate with them rather than compete (win-win).

  • ~70% stay 3+ nights (42M qualified tourists)
  • ~40% would use a breakfast service (16.8M potential customers)
  • Average spend: $75-150 per trip on breakfast
  • TAM = 16.8M √ó $100 average = $1.68B conservative, up to $12B if we capture repeat usage

This doesn't include VRBO, Booking.com, and other vacation rental platforms, which could double the TAM.

Which markets are you targeting first?

We're launching in high-tourist-density cities with strong food cultures:

Phase 1 (Months 1-6): 3-5 Cities

  • Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
  • Miami, USA
  • Barcelona, Spain
  • Bali, Indonesia

Selection Criteria:

  • High vacation rental density
  • Strong local food culture
  • English + local language support
  • Favorable regulations
  • Year-round tourism (not just seasonal)
What about seasonality?

Tourism has natural seasonality, but we mitigate this through:

  • Geographic Diversification: Launching in cities across different hemispheres (counter-seasonal)
  • Business Travelers: Secondary target segment with consistent year-round demand
  • Local Residents: Providers can serve local customers during low tourist seasons
  • Strategic Timing: Launch cities are selected for year-round tourism appeal

Operations & Execution

How do you ensure food quality and safety?

Quality and safety are paramount:

  1. Provider Verification: All providers must have local business licenses and food safety certifications
  2. Rating System: Tourist reviews and ratings with quality thresholds
  3. Insurance: Platform carries liability insurance for food safety incidents
  4. Regular Audits: Random quality checks and provider reviews
  5. Clear Standards: Published guidelines for preparation, packaging, and delivery
How do you acquire tourists (customers)?

Pre-Trip Channels:

  • SEO/Content: "Breakfast in [city]" searches, travel blogs
  • Social Media: Instagram/TikTok with local food influencers
  • Partnerships: Property managers, Airbnb hosts including in welcome packets
  • Paid Ads: Google Ads, Facebook targeting vacation planners

Word-of-Mouth:

  • Referral program (give $10, get $10)
  • Provider recommendations to their customers
  • Social sharing incentives
How do you recruit breakfast providers?

Provider acquisition is straightforward:

  1. Direct Outreach: Local team visits cafes, bakeries, home cooks
  2. Value Proposition: Access to tourist market without storefront overhead
  3. Low Barrier: Anyone with food license can join (home-based businesses welcome)
  4. Marketing Support: We provide photography, menu templates, training
  5. Guaranteed Payment: We handle all payment processing and disputes

Initial feedback: Providers are eager to join - tourists are high-value customers with predictable orders.

What's your technology advantage?

We've built vacation-specific technology from the ground up:

  • Recurring Order Engine: Handles multi-day scheduled deliveries (not available in standard food delivery apps)
  • Pre-Trip Booking: Tourists book before traveling, with flexible modification options
  • Matching Algorithm: Considers location, dietary restrictions, cuisine preferences, provider capacity
  • Multi-Language: Support for both tourist and provider languages
  • Vacation Context: Integration with travel dates, accommodation addresses, etc.
